## üöÄ Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit the Registration Page**
   - Click "Start Free Trial" or "Get Started" on the homepage
   - Or navigate directly to the registration page

2. **Sign Up**
   - Enter your email address
   - Create a secure password (minimum 8 characters)
   - Provide your name
   - Click "Register"

3. **Verify Your Account**
   - Check your email for verification (if required)
   - Log in with your credentials

### Adding Your First Medication

1. **Navigate to Medications**
   - Click "Medications" in the sidebar or use the "Add Medication" button

2. **Fill in Medication Details**
   - **Medication Name**: Enter the name of your medication
   - **Dosage**: Specify the dose (e.g., 20mg)
   - **Form**: Select the form (tablet, capsule, liquid, etc.)
   - **Quantity**: Enter how many units per dose
   - **Units**: Select the unit type (pill, ml, etc.)

3. **Set Up Schedule**
   - **Start Date**: When you begin taking this medication
   - **End Date**: When you'll stop (optional for ongoing medications)
   - **Frequency**: How often you take it (daily, specific days of week)
   - **Times**: Select times of day (morning, afternoon, evening, or custom)
   - **Meal Timing**: When to take relative to meals (before, with, after, anytime)

4. **Save**
   - Review your information
   - Click "Save" to create your medication schedule

### Using Your Dashboard

Your dashboard provides a quick overview of your medication management:

- **Next Medication**: See what medication you need to take next, with time remaining or overdue status
- **Daily Progress**: Track how many medications you've taken today
- **Statistics**: View adherence rate, trends, day streak, and missed medications
- **Quick Actions**: Access common tasks like adding medications or viewing your full schedule

### Marking Medications as Taken

1. **From Dashboard**
   - Click the "Take" button on the "Next Medication" card

2. **From Today View**
   - Click the checkbox or "Take" button next to any medication
   - The medication will be marked as completed

3. **From Week View**
   - Click on any scheduled medication entry
   - Mark it as taken from the detail view

### Viewing Your Schedule

- **Today View**: See all medications scheduled for today with the ability to mark them as taken
- **Week View**: View your complete weekly schedule in a calendar format
- **Schedule Management**: Edit, pause, or delete schedules as needed

## üìñ User Guide

### Managing Medications

#### Adding a New Medication

1. Go to **Medications** ‚Üí **Add Medication**
2. Complete the medication wizard:
   - Step 1: Basic information (name, dosage, form)
   - Step 2: Quantity and units
   - Step 3: Schedule details (dates, frequency, times)
   - Step 4: Meal timing preferences
3. Review and save

#### Editing a Medication

1. Go to **Medications**
2. Find the medication you want to edit
3. Click "Edit"
4. Update any information
5. Save changes

#### Deleting a Medication

1. Go to **Medications**
2. Find the medication
3. Click "Delete"
4. Confirm deletion

**Note**: Deleting a medication will remove all future associated schedules but will keep the history.

### Managing Schedules

#### Creating a Schedule

Schedules are automatically created when you add a medication, but you can also:

1. Go to **Schedule**
2. Click "Add Schedule"
3. Select a medication
4. Configure schedule details
5. Save

#### Editing a Schedule

1. Go to **Schedule**
2. Find the schedule you want to edit
3. Click "Edit"
4. Modify schedule details (times, frequency, dates)
5. Save changes

#### Viewing Schedule History

- All past schedule entries are preserved
- View historical data in the Week View
- Track adherence patterns over time

### Sharing Your Schedule

#### Sharing with a Caregiver

1. Go to **Data Sharing** or **Share**
2. Click "Generate Share Link"
3. Set an expiration date (optional)
4. Copy the share link
5. Send the link to your caregiver via email, text, or messaging app

#### Accepting a Share Link

1. Click on the share link you received
2. Log in or create an account
3. The shared schedule will appear in your "Profiles I can view" section

#### Revoking Access

1. Go to **Data Sharing**
2. Find the person you shared with
3. Click "Revoke Access"
4. Confirm revocation

### Managing Dependents

#### Adding a Dependent

1. Go to **Dependents**
2. Click "Add Dependent"
3. Enter their information:
   - Name
   - Date of birth (optional)
   - Relationship
4. Save

#### Managing Medications for Dependents

1. Go to **Dependents**
2. Select the dependent
3. Add medications and schedules as you would for yourself
4. All schedules will be associated with the dependent

### Viewing Statistics

#### Dashboard Statistics

- **Adherence Rate**: Percentage of medications taken as scheduled
- **Trend**: Changes in adherence over time
- **Day Streak**: Consecutive days of perfect adherence
- **Missed**: Count of missed medications

#### Weekly Overview

- View adherence for the current week
- See planned vs. taken medications
- Identify patterns and areas for improvement

### Exporting Your Schedule

#### PDF Export

1. Go to **Week View**
2. Click "Print" or "Export PDF"
3. Your schedule will be generated as a PDF
4. Download and save or print

**Use Cases**:

- Share with healthcare providers
- Keep a physical copy
- Reference when offline

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is Prescription Clarity free to use?**  
A: We offer a free 30-day trial. After that, choose from our subscription plans that fit your needs.

**Q: Can I use Prescription Clarity on my phone?**  
A: Yes! Prescription Clarity is fully responsive and works on all devices - desktop, tablet, and mobile phones.

**Q: Do I need to download an app?**  
A: No, Prescription Clarity is a web application. Simply visit the website in your browser - no downloads required.

**Q: Can multiple people manage the same medications?**  
A: Yes! Use the sharing feature to allow caregivers or family members to view and help manage medications.

### Medication Management

**Q: Can I edit a medication after adding it?**  
A: Yes, you can edit any medication at any time. Changes will update future schedule entries.

**Q: What happens if I delete a medication?**  
A: Deleting a medication removes it and all associated schedules. Historical data is preserved for reference.

**Q: Can I schedule medications for different times on different days?**  
A: Currently, schedules use consistent times. For complex schedules, you may need to create multiple schedule entries.

### Sharing & Access

**Q: How secure is the sharing feature?**  
A: Share links use secure tokens and can be set to expire. You can revoke access at any time.

**Q: Can I see who has access to my schedule?**  
A: Yes, go to "Data Sharing" to see all people who have access to your schedule.

**Q: What can people with share access see?**  
A: Viewers can see your medication schedule and adherence data, but cannot make changes.

**Q: Can I share with my doctor?**  
A: Yes! Generate a share link and send it to your healthcare provider. They can view your schedule with your permission.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What browsers are supported?**  
A: Prescription Clarity works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge.

**Q: Do I need an internet connection?**  
A: Yes, Prescription Clarity is a web-based platform. However, you can export your schedule as a PDF for offline reference.

**Q: How do I reset my password?**  
A: Use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page. You'll receive instructions via email.

**Q: Can I export my data?**  
A: Yes, you can export your schedule as a PDF. Contact support for full data export options.
